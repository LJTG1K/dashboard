# The Grand Tour — Execution Strategy

---

## Phase 1: Research & Targeting (Weeks 1-2)

### 1.1 Identify Target Sellers

**Source:** Taobao, 1688, WeChat seller networks

**Criteria for ideal sellers:**
- ✅ **Fashion/apparel focused** (aligns with RAPID audience)
- ✅ **10k-500k followers** (established, but not too big to negotiate)
- ✅ **Fast shipping capability** (2-3 day prep for international orders)
- ✅ **English or willingness to communicate** (easier pitch)
- ✅ **Quality reputation** (reviews 4.5+ stars)

**Priority categories:**
1. Streetwear/hiphop brands (largest RAPID audience)
2. Designer replicas/luxury (high margin)
3. Niche fashion (unique inventory = exclusive deals)
4. Sportswear (high volume)

### 1.2 Build Target List

**Create spreadsheet with:**
- Seller name / shop name
- Platform (1688 / Taobao / WeChat)
- Follower count
- Category
- Email / WeChat / contact
- Notes (why we want them)
- Contact attempt date

**Target: 50+ sellers by region**
- Shanghai (10-15 sellers)
- Shenzhen (10-15 sellers)
- Guangzhou (10-15 sellers)
- Hangzhou (10-15 sellers)
- Other (5-10 sellers)

### 1.3 Initial Outreach

**Before visiting, warm up the relationship:**
1. Follow on WeChat/social
2. Send friendly message (translated): "Hi! We love your products. Would love to chat about a partnership opportunity when we're in [city] next month."
3. Don't ask for anything yet—just get their attention
4. Wait for response before scheduling

## Phase 2: Pre-Tour Prep (Weeks 2-3)

### 2.1 Logistics

**Book accommodations near seller clusters:**
- Airbnb / boutique hotels with good WiFi
- Close to metro (easy to reach seller locations)

**Book flights:**
- Suggest: Fly into Shanghai, train to Guangzhou, fly to Shenzhen, return from Beijing
- Or: Circular route based on seller density

**Arrange translator:**
- Native Mandarin speaker + basic English
- Ideally: Someone familiar with e-commerce/fashion industry
- Availability: Full-time during tour (10-14 days)

### 2.2 Pitch Materials

**Prepare in advance:**
- [ ] Printed pitch deck (30-50 copies in both English & Mandarin)
- [ ] RAPID product catalog (printed or on phone)
- [ ] Sample contract templates (exclusive deal & ad partnership)
- [ ] Business cards (English/Mandarin bilingual)
- [ ] Tablet/laptop for live demos
- [ ] QR code linking to RAPID platform
- [ ] Testimonial video (if possible) from early partners

### 2.3 Legal Prep

**Draft simple contracts:**
1. **Exclusive Deal Template**
   - Seller provides exclusive products/pricing
   - RAPID sells, splits profit 50/60/40 (define)
   - Duration: 3-6 months (trial period)
   - Cancellation terms: 30 days notice

2. **Ad Partnership Template**
   - Seller pays $X/month
   - RAPID provides featured placement for X days
   - Performance metrics (impressions, clicks, orders)
   - Cancellation: Monthly, no lock-in

3. **Standard Listing Agreement**
   - Seller provides catalog + photos
   - RAPID lists on platform
   - Revenue split: 10-15% commission per sale
   - Payment: Monthly payouts
   - Cancellation: Anytime

**Have local lawyer review** before printing.

### 2.4 Pre-Meeting Confirmation

**2 weeks before visit:**
- Send formal calendar invite (in Chinese) with date/time/location
- Confirm meeting 3 days prior
- Get backup contact (if main person unavailable)
- Send pitch deck preview via WeChat

## Phase 3: The Tour (Weeks 3-4)

### 3.1 Meeting Structure (45 minutes per seller)

**Before arrival (5 min):**
- Small talk (tea, coffee, compliments on their shop)
- Build rapport
- Explain translator is here to ensure accuracy

**The Pitch (10 min):**
- Quick overview using slides
- Focus on benefits to THEM, not us
- Live demo on tablet (show their potential products on RAPID)

**Discussion (20 min):**
- Listen more than talk
- Ask: "What are your biggest challenges with international sales?"
- Ask: "What would make RAPID valuable to you?"
- Address concerns (payment, shipping, language)
- Gauge interest (exclusive deal vs. ad partnership vs. just listing)

**Close (10 min):**
- Explain next steps clearly
- Leave contract + business card
- Get email/WeChat for follow-up
- Take photo for documentation

### 3.2 Daily Routine

**Morning:**
- Review meetings for the day (3-4 max)
- Prep any customizations to pitch (if you learned something useful yesterday)
- Confirm all meetings

**Afternoon/Evening:**
- Document notes from meetings (seller interest level, products, contacts)
- Update tracking spreadsheet
- Prep tomorrow's presentations
- Rest (meeting people is exhausting)

### 3.3 What to Look For

**Red flags (skip these sellers):**
- Unwilling to share product catalog
- Defensive about quality/authenticity
- Only interested in cash upfront (payment scam risk)
- Can't ship within 2-3 days

**Green lights (pursue aggressively):**
- Excited about global reach
- Already using agents (Sugargoo/CSSBuy) = understand logistics
- Large existing inventory (easy to add)
- Professional demeanor (reliable partner)

### 3.4 Handling Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I don't speak English" | "That's fine—RAPID handles customer service. You never touch it." |
| "How do I get paid?" | "Monthly bank transfer in RMB. No fees." (show payment example) |
| "Will shipping be expensive?" | "Sugargoo negotiates volume rates. Usually $2-5 per item." |
| "What if customers complain?" | "RAPID/Sugargoo handles all complaints. You're not involved." |
| "Why should I trust you?" | "Start small—10 products. See the results. Decide after 30 days." |
| "Other platforms offer better deals" | "Maybe. But do they reach 2M fashion enthusiasts? Come back in 60 days and see your sales data." |

## Phase 4: Follow-Up (Weeks 5-6)

### 4.1 Immediate Follow-Up (Within 3 days)

**For every seller you met:**
1. Send thank you message via WeChat (translated)
2. Send contract via email (both English & Mandarin)
3. Include 2-3 questions from the meeting (show you were listening)
4. Provide direct contact for questions

**Template message:**
> "Hi [Seller name], Thank you for meeting with us! Your [product category] are exactly what RAPID customers want. We've sent the [exclusive deal / ad partnership] contract. No rush—please review and let us know if you have questions. We're excited to work with you!"

### 4.2 Conversion Tracking

**Create follow-up timeline:**
- Day 3: Send contract
- Day 10: Check in (gentle reminder)
- Day 17: Call/WeChat (if no response)
- Day 24: Final follow-up
- Day 30: Mark as lost or convert

**Track conversion metrics:**
- Meetings conducted
- Contracts sent
- Contracts signed
- Products added to RAPID
- Revenue generated

### 4.3 Onboarding Signed Sellers

**Once contract is signed:**
1. Get product catalog (photos, descriptions, prices)
2. Build products in RAPID database
3. Set up commission/payment split in system
4. Go live on platform
5. Monitor first 30 days closely (success = ongoing partnership)

## Deal Structures (Examples)

### Deal Type A: Exclusive Products

**Scenario:** Seller has unique hoodie designs, only available on their 1688 shop.

**Offer:**
- "Give us 10 exclusive hoodies (wholesale pricing)"
- "We sell them on RAPID at retail"
- "We split profit 50/50"

**Math example:**
- Wholesale cost: 100 RMB per hoodie
- Retail price on RAPID: $30 USD (≈200 RMB)
- Sales price in RMB: 1,500 RMB per hoodie (including shipping)
- Your profit: 750 RMB per hoodie
- RAPID's profit: 750 RMB per hoodie
- **Both win**

### Deal Type B: Revenue Share on Existing Catalog

**Scenario:** Seller already has 500 SKUs, wants to sell internationally.

**Offer:**
- "List your catalog on RAPID"
- "We drive traffic, handle payments/shipping"
- "We keep 10-15% per sale"

**Math example:**
- Seller lists 500 products
- Average order: $50 USD
- Commission: 10% = $5 USD per sale
- If 20 sales/day: $100 USD commission

### Deal Type C: Advertising/Promotion

**Scenario:** Seller wants guaranteed visibility, willing to pay.

**Offer:**
- "Featured spot on RAPID homepage for 30 days"
- "We drive at least 1,000 visitors to your products"
- "Cost: $1,500 USD (≈10,000 RMB)"

**Value to seller:**
- 1,000 visits × 3% conversion = 30 orders
- Average order: $100 = $3,000 revenue
- Profit margin: 40% = $1,200 profit
- **ROI: 80% (paid $1,500, made $1,200 profit)**

## Key Success Factors

1. **Respect their time** — Show up early, be professional, end on time
2. **Listen more than talk** — Ask good questions, understand their business
3. **Start small** — Don't oversell. "Try 10 products, see results, then scale."
4. **Be transparent** — Clear about fees, payment terms, expectations
5. **Follow through** — Every seller who signs gets fast, professional onboarding
6. **Measure everything** — Track conversions, revenue, satisfaction
